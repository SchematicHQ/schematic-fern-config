This page describes how Customer Data moves between your application and Schematic, where Schematic stores it, and the controls that protect it. For the canonical subprocessor list, see [Subprocessors](https://schematichq.com/subprocessors). For the legal commitments around processing, see the [Data Processing Addendum](https://schematichq.com/dpa).

## What we mean by Customer Data

Customer Data is the information you submit to Schematic for processing on your behalf. It includes the identity keys your systems use to refer to a company or user, optional profile traits (name, email, role, or any custom field you send), usage events recorded through the `track` API, and the subscription state Schematic mirrors from your billing provider.

Schematic does not store, process, or transmit payment card data. Card data is handled by your billing provider. Stripe, our primary integration, is PCI Level 1 certified.

## Where Schematic stores Customer Data

All Schematic services run in AWS `us-east-1`, multi-AZ. The data classes you submit land in the stores below.

| Data class | Store | Location | Encryption at rest | Retention |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Companies, users, plans, features, entitlements, overrides, subscription mirror | Managed Postgres | AWS `us-east-1` | AES-256 | Lifetime of account; deletion within 30 days of request |
| Usage events and rollups | ClickHouse Cloud | AWS `us-east-1` | AES-256 | 60 days default, up to 1 year on enterprise plans |
| Audit log entries | ClickHouse Cloud | AWS `us-east-1` | AES-256 | Two years |
| Backups and snapshots | Amazon S3, AWS RDS snapshots | AWS `us-east-1` | AES-256 | Continuous PITR |

Customer Data is currently processed in the United States. Multi-region support is on the roadmap.

## How Schematic protects your data

### Encryption

All customer-facing endpoints require TLS 1.2 or higher, terminated at the load balancer. Data at rest is encrypted with AES-256 across managed Postgres, ClickHouse Cloud, and Amazon S3 backups. API keys and service credentials live in encrypted secret stores and are never written to logs. Outbound webhooks are signed, and every Schematic SDK includes helpers for verifying webhook signatures before your application processes them.

### Access control

API access is segmented by environment and key type. Publishable keys are scoped for client-side SDKs and carry limited authority. Secret keys are used by server-side SDKs and grant the access required to call privileged endpoints. Embedded components render with short-lived JWT session tokens minted on your server, which avoids exposing secret keys to the browser.

The Schematic dashboard authenticates through SSO and applies role-based access control by environment and resource type. Privileged actions are written to an audit log retained in ClickHouse for two years.

### Network and infrastructure

Schematic runs in AWS `us-east-1` across multiple availability zones. Application services and data stores live inside a private VPC with security-group isolation, and no data store is exposed to the public internet. AWS GuardDuty and AWS Security Hub provide continuous threat detection and security posture monitoring. Vulnerability scanning runs continuously.

### Compliance

Schematic holds a SOC 2 Type II report covering the Security and Availability Trust Service Criteria. PCI compliance is handled by your billing provider; Schematic is out of scope because it does not touch card data. Every subprocessor that processes Customer Data is bound by a data processing agreement with protections substantiecurally equivalent to Schematic's own commitments to customers, and the subprocessor list is published with at least 30 days of notice before any addition.

Schematic supports data subject access and deletion requests in line with GDPR and CCPA. Deletion requests are completed within 30 days of confirmation.

For more information, see our [Trust Center](https://trust.schematichq.com)

### Resilience and recovery

Schematic targets 99.99% uptime, with SLA credits available to customers on contracts that include them. Continuous point-in-time backups run against managed Postgres, and the recovery procedure has been validated end-to-end. The database stays small relative to infrastructure capacity because high-volume event data is offloaded to ClickHouse Cloud.

On the customer side, every Schematic SDK supports configurable fallback values, so your application can decide what features look like during a Schematic outage. Customers who need stronger isolation can run [Schematic Replicator](https://github.com/schematicHQ/schematic-replicator) inside their own infrastructure to serve entitlements locally with no API dependency on Schematic during a degraded period.

### Incident response

Customers are notified within 72 hours of a confirmed incident affecting their Customer Data, in line with the Data Processing Addendum.
